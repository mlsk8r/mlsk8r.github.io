Methodology

Research

Knowing Where You Are. In order to move forward, you have to recognize and acknowledge where you are. Knowing where you are helps you plan for the future and begin to build momentum and efficiency for your project. Early research and planning can include a number of things from existing site analytics to competitive analysis. Often, new ideas or the smallest of details discovered in research during this stage result in the biggest gains for the project moving forward.

Design

User Experience & Feeback. Designs and prototypes should reinforce good user experiences and promote accessibility. All while speaking to your targeted audience and providing well-developed personality. Constructive feedback and user centered testing can spark ideas and team collaboration. This process is important and is the nexus for all future designs and prototypes. Designs sometimes only need a single round of feedback, other times two or three. If designs feel ambiguous and uncertain, an A/B test may be recommended to help decide.

Develop

Modern UI/UX Development. Projects often require a variety of technologies to provide the ideal layout and user experience. Well supported libraries, frameworks, and services are used for a complete system. Some common development tools used in these projects are HTML5, CSS3, SCSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, Grid Frameworks, content management systems, Google Analytics, A/B Testing, and more. Working in collaboration with the entire team and employing every individual talent and skill available for development is what makes this stage successful. Seeing the merger of Design and Technical can be an exciting process during this time.

Optomize

Moving Forward. The best part of launching a new product is watching it grow. All projects and products involving user interaction are organic and change, nothing is ever “final”. Using solid analytics, further user testing can be conducted to ensure that the project and user experience are moving in the right direction.
