Understanding where you are

In order to move forward, you have to recognize and acknowledge where you are. Knowing where you are helps you plan for the future and begin to build momentum and efficiency for your project. Early research and planning can include a number of things from existing site analytics to competitive analysis. Often, new ideas or the smallest of details discovered in research during this stage result in the biggest gains for the project moving forward.

User experience & feedback

Wireframes, user flows, mockup designs, and prototypes should reinforce good user experiences and promote accessibility. Constructive feedback and user centered testing can spark ideas and team collaboration. This process is important and is the nexus for all future design iterations and prototypes. Designs sometimes only need a single round of feedback, other times two or three. Being flexible and open to new ideas is important during this process.

Modern UX/UI development

Projects often require a variety of technologies to provide the ideal layout and user experience. Well supported libraries, frameworks, and services are used for a complete system. Some common design and development tools used in these projects are: Adobe XD, HTML5, CSS3, GIT, JavaScript, Bootstrap, Grid Frameworks, A/B Testing, and more. Working in collaboration with the entire team is what makes this stage successful.

Progression

The best part of launching a new product is watching it grow. All projects and products involving user interaction are organic and change, nothing is ever “final”. Using solid analytics, further user testing can be conducted to ensure that the project and user experience are moving in the right direction.
