Description of Application or Project

{{::findAttrByKey(c.data.attributes, "Description of Application or Project")}}
The requesting product is the OnPrem missionary data warehouse; it will store the data incoming from OPI and TrueNorth that is needed by the missionary department.The data warehouse is the database for all available missionary-related data. It has inputs from various sources, including Area Book Planner, Ed App, and with this DSA, data from the MTC (OPI and TrueNorth).

Necessity of Data

{{::findAttrByKey(c.data.attributes, "Necessity of Data")}}
The Language Learning tests that missionaries are going to have while on their missions will be overseen by the MTC. The data will help mission presidents understand the proficiency of missionaries learning new languages. Data will be provided at an aggregated level.

Scope and Control

{{::findAttrByKey(c.data.attributes, "Scope and Control")}}
Access to Raw data will be limited to the warehousing team (ICS/BI and missionary reporting team) will bring the data in and model it for consumption in reports; the data will be reported at an aggregate level per mission. Accesses and distribution of the reports created will be to Mission leadership, including Area Mission Specialists, Area Presidencies, Mission Presidents, and missionary department internal staff. Data will be stored during a missionaries tenure and as needed for aggregate comparison for the term of this agreement.

Additional Information Requested

{{::findAttrByKey(c.data.attributes, "Additional Information Requested")}}

I was not able to find a work location description, job title, weekly compensation rate, normal hours worked, and payroll tax state. This information is used by our third party claims administrator to compensate BYU employees who have work related injuries.

Per email from Luke Rowley on 3 Mar 2020:

Work Location is found in the Address Table with an Address Type = WRK.

Job Title will come in the job_code Object.

Weekly Compensation Rate can be caluclated based on the compensation_rate and the pay_frequency in the Compensations fieldset. o The consuming system would need to divide the Annual rate by 52. If the compensation_rate_frequency is "Hourly", then you would take the compensation_rate and multiply it by the hours worked in a week.

Normal Hours Worked and Payroll Tax State are not currently available, but discussions are being held to add this data in the future.
