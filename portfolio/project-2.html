Commencement Website Rebuild

The University of Arizona

Project Overview

The UA hired J.F.

TLDR

Complete site rebuild from start to finish using Drupal CMS

Collaborated with and managed the project with multiple departments and stakeholders

Moved Content Management from out-of-date system to new Drupal CMS build

Created workflows for backend users to manage and maintain site content without needing web development knowledge

Trained content editors on how to use the CMS and maintain the site content

The Process

The previous Commencement website was outdated and needed a complete redesign and rebuild. The site wasn’t built in a proper content management system and was difficult to maintain if the person doing the maintenance was not a web developer. The site needed to be built in Drupal CMS with a back-end that allowed content editors and novice users to make simple edits.

No project in higher education involving a website is created in a vacuum. In order for this project to be successful, meetings were held often with department heads and stakeholders involved in commencement. This allowed for the creation of specific project goals that could stay within the scope of resources available and timeline for project delivery.

The previous commencement website was built in an outdated content management system that didn’t allow for user-friendly management or updating. The website was rebuilt using Drupal as the new content management system and optimized for the best experience for content managers.

The commencement website needed to have an easy-to-use backend environment for content editors with very little web development and design knowledge. Workflows and components were developed on the backend of Drupal with a user-first mentality. The goal was to make editing and maintaining the website as easy as possible for future content editors without needing to make changes to any code.

After developing and testing the user experience of the backend editing tools of Drupal, the final website was ready for Phase I launch. Prior to the website being launched, several training sessions were held with the commencement staff who would be responsible to update and maintain the website. Training sessions on how to use the workflows and editing tools in drupal were facilitated and editors were allowed time to test, train, and ask questions regarding the tools, components, and workflows. Issues regarding the ease-of-use of the tools for editors were quickly discovered and fixed after several training sessions. After the launch of the new website, followup was conducted regarding any issues or extra training for content editors and the continued maintenance and editing of the commencement website was handed over to the commencement department.
